Four phases, and the people who carry them from drawing to daily use.

A project with us is not a transaction for a product. It is a set of decisions made in the right order, by the right people, starting at the architectural drawings and ending at the first quiet evening in the finished room.

Below is the full arc of that work. Most projects pass through all four phases. A retrofit may begin at the second. A service call starts at the fourth. The steps are the same either way, and the same names appear on them.

§ 01 of 04 01

Design.
Drawings first, in that room.

Before anything is specified, we sit with the architect's plans, the interior designer's palette, and you. The outcome of this phase is a set of drawings the house will be built against, not retrofitted to.

What happens

A walk-through of the site if it exists, or a conversation over the architect's set if it doesn't. We ask how the house is used, where the art is going, where the kids sleep, how you take your morning. Then we draw.

Lighting layers, shade pockets, speaker placements, display locations, media closets, and cable paths are drawn to scale and shared back for review. Two rounds is typical.

What you receive

  • Lighting plan, to scale
  • Shade schedule by window
  • Speaker and display layout
  • Cable pathway drawings
  • Media closet elevation
  • Keypad placement plan
  • Signed cover sheet
§ 02 of 04 02

Engineer.
Specify, schedule, and coordinate.

Drawings become a schedule of every part that will enter the house, with model numbers, finishes, engraving, quantities, lead times, and the trade responsible for each. This is the document the project is actually built from.

What happens

The fixture schedule, the shade schedule, and the control schedule are written line by line. Keypads are engraved, finishes are sampled, cable counts are confirmed against the drawings. We pre-wire dates with the electrician and rough-in dates with the builder.

Long-lead items are ordered in this phase. Everything is staged at our shop before it arrives on site.

What you receive

  • Fixture schedule, itemized
  • Shade and fabric approval
  • Keypad engraving proofs
  • Load schedule with circuits
  • Coordination letters to trades
  • Fixed-price line-item proposal
  • Site-walk date on the calendar
§ 03 of 04 03

Install.
By the hands that drew it.

Rough-in, pre-wire, trim-out, programming, calibration, and the final walk-through. Every cable in the walls is pulled by someone on our payroll. The technician who finishes the rough-in is the technician who hands you the keypad.

What happens

Pre-wire rides the electrician's rough-in, not after it. Speakers and keypads go in when the drywall is still open. Displays are recessed into their boxes before paint. Programming happens in our shop against a copy of the house's actual address list, then loaded on site.

A calibration pass closes the phase. The microphones come out, the scenes are named, the control app is handed over.

What you receive

  • Rough-in inspection sign-off
  • Programmed keypads, labeled
  • Calibration report per room
  • Control app provisioned
  • Scene library, written plain
  • As-built drawings, archived
  • Closing walk-through with notes
§ 04 of 04 04

Service.
The years after the last trim screw.

Houses change. Habits change. Firmware changes. The fourth phase begins the day the crew packs out, and runs for as long as the system is on the wall. You keep the phone number, and the same people who built it keep the house.

What happens

An annual visit is scheduled before we leave. We check the system against its original calibration, update firmware where it's earned it, and replace anything that has drifted out of tolerance. Between visits, we answer the phone.

Additions, renovations, and room-for-room replacements all start here. The plan you already own gets amended, not rewritten.

What you receive

  • Annual service visit, scheduled
  • Priority phone line
  • Firmware maintenance
  • Parts warranty support
  • Live portal with your drawings
  • Same-week response in-region
  • A number, not a ticket system

Warranty & support

We are a phone number, not a ticket queue.

Every project we deliver carries a two-year parts-and-labor warranty from the day of final walk-through. Manufacturers' coverage continues beyond that, administered by us so you are not on the phone with a call center in another time zone.

If something stops working, the same team that installed it answers. For anything urgent, we aim to be on site within the business day. For everything else, we schedule around your calendar.
