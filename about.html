We design beautiful spaces, and curate the technology inside them.

What we do is residential, and the scope is quiet: lighting, shading, audio, video, and the control that ties them together. Drawings follow the architecture. Every line on those drawings is something we've earned the right to specify, and every install is finished by people on our payroll.

Three generations of one family have run this work since 1979, out of the same town west of Orlando. Growth has been deliberately slow. The project count stays where it does because the people who draw the plan are the ones who answer the phone two years later.

How we work

Three things we keep in the house.

§ 01

The plan.

Lighting, shading, cable paths, media closets, speakers in ceilings, keypads in walls, all of it appears on a set of drawings before a single fixture is ordered. The pages carry our name at the bottom.

§ 02

The spec.

A room asks for particular things, and the catalog is rarely the answer. We choose for the space in front of us, say why, and will tell you when something you've seen elsewhere would land better in your house than the thing you assumed you wanted.

§ 03

The install.

Every cable in the walls is pulled by someone on our payroll. The person who finishes the rough-in is the person you see again for a software update, a calibration pass, or a media room added two years later.
